What is this medicine?

Zenoxetine CR contains Paroxetine, a medicine belonging to a group called Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs). It is one of the most widely prescribed and well-studied medicines for mood, anxiety, and related conditions. The 'CR' (Controlled Release) formulation releases the medicine slowly through the day, which helps reduce stomach-related side effects compared to regular tablets.

What is it used for?

Doctors commonly prescribe this medicine for:

  • Depression and persistent low mood
  • Generalised Anxiety Disorder (excessive worry)
  • Panic Disorder (sudden episodes of intense fear)
  • Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
  • Social Anxiety Disorder
  • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

How does it work?

Serotonin is a natural chemical messenger in the brain that helps regulate mood, sleep, and anxiety. In many mental health conditions, serotonin signalling is reduced. Paroxetine works by blocking the reuptake (re-absorption) of serotonin in the brain, allowing more of it to remain available between nerve cells. Over time, this helps restore a more balanced mood and reduces excessive anxiety.

How to take this medicine

  • Take exactly as prescribed by your doctor — usually once a day, in the morning, with or after food.
  • Swallow the tablet whole with a glass of water. Do not crush, chew, or split the controlled-release tablet, as this affects how the medicine is released.
  • It is common to feel little or no difference in the first 1–2 weeks. Most people notice meaningful improvement over 2–6 weeks. Continue taking it even if you don't feel an immediate change.
  • Do not stop this medicine suddenly on your own, even if you feel better. Stopping abruptly can cause withdrawal-like symptoms (dizziness, irritability, sleep disturbance). Your doctor will guide you on tapering off gradually when the time is right.

Possible side effects

Like all medicines, this can cause side effects, although not everyone gets them. Most are mild and improve as your body adjusts.

Nausea (usually settles in 1-2 weeks) Dry mouth Drowsiness or trouble sleeping Sweating Reduced appetite Changes in sexual desire/function Mild dizziness

Important safety information

  • Do not combine with alcohol or other sedating medicines unless your doctor approves.
  • Inform your doctor about ALL other medicines you take, especially other antidepressants, migraine medicines (triptans), blood thinners, or pain medicines (NSAIDs) — combinations can increase risk of side effects.
  • Never combine with MAOI antidepressants — this combination can be dangerous. Leave a gap of at least 2 weeks between switching.
  • Inform your doctor if you are pregnant, planning pregnancy, or breastfeeding.
  • Contact your doctor immediately if you experience unusual changes in mood, thoughts of self-harm, or worsening anxiety, especially in the first few weeks of treatment or after a dose change — this applies particularly to younger patients.
  • Use caution while driving or operating machinery until you know how this medicine affects you.

Storage

Store below 30°C, in a dry place, away from direct sunlight. Keep the strip in its original packaging until use. Keep out of reach and sight of children.
